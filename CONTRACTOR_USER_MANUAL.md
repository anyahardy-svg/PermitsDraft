## Getting Started

### System Requirements

- **Web Browser:** Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge (latest version recommended)
- **Mobile:** iOS or Android device with Expo Go app installed
- **Internet Connection:** Required for all operations
- **PDF Support:** System supports PDF and image uploads

### First Time Access

1. You'll receive an **invitation email** with a link to create your account
2. Click the link in the email - this takes you to the registration page
3. Create a secure password (minimum 8 characters recommended)
4. You're ready to start using ContractorHQ!

## Logging In

### Web Access (Recommended)

1. Go to the ContractorHQ portal in your web browser
2. Enter your **email address**
3. Enter your **password**

5. You'll be directed to your dashboard

1. On the login page, click **"Forgot Password?"**

4. Click the link and create a new password

- The system can remember your login for up to **30 minutes** of inactivity on desktop
- Mobile/tablet sessions clear when you refresh the browser
- For security, always **log out** when using shared devices

## Managing Permits

### What is a Permit to Work?

A Permit to Work (PTW) is an official document that authorizes specific work to be done at a specific location under controlled conditions. It ensures:
- Work is properly planned
- Hazards are identified
- Appropriate controls are in place
- Work is authorized before beginning

### Creating a New Permit

#### Step 1: Start a New Permit

1. Go to the **Permits** tab
2. Click **"+ Create New Permit"**
3. Select the **permit type** for your work:
   - General Work
   - Hot Work (welding, cutting, grinding)
   - Confined Space Entry
   - Working at Height
   - Electrical Work
   - Excavation & Demolition
   - Lifting Operations
   - Other specialized permits

#### Step 2: Enter Work Details

Fill in the following information:

| Field | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Work Description** | What work will be done? | "Welding pipeline section PL-12" |
| **Site/Location** | Where will work occur? | "Main plant, Building A" |
| **Requested By** | Who requested this work? | "John Smith" |
| **Department/Team** | Your team or business unit | "Maintenance" |
| **Date & Time** | When will work start? | Today, 08:00 AM |
| **Estimated Duration** | How long will it take? | 4 hours |

#### Step 3: Answer Safety Questions

The system will ask specific questions based on your permit type. These might include:

**For Hot Work:**
- "Can this work be done in the workshop?"
- "Are fire extinguishers available?"
- "Is atmospheric testing required?"

**For Confined Space:**
- "Are all isolations completed?"
- "Are workers trained for confined space entry?"
- "Is ventilation equipment available?"

For each question:
- Select **"Yes"** or **"No"** 
- Add details in text fields if required
- If you're unsure, click the **"?"** icon for guidance

#### Step 4: Identify Hazards & Controls

1. **Hazards:** What could go wrong? (e.g., "Burns from hot metal")
2. **Controls:** How will you prevent it? (e.g., "Wear fire-resistant clothing")
3. Click **"Add Hazard"** for each one
4. Rate the risk level (Low, Medium, High, Critical)

#### Step 5: Save Your Draft

- Click **"Save as Draft"** - you can edit this later
- Your permit appears in the "Draft Permits" section with a **red border**

### Working with Saved Permits

#### Edit a Draft Permit

1. Find the permit in your **Draft Permits** list
2. Click on it to open it
3. Make your changes
4. Click **"Save Changes"**

#### Submit for Approval

Once your permit is complete and reviewed:

1. Open the draft permit
2. Click **"Submit for Approval"**
3. Add any final notes (optional)
4. Click **"Confirm Submit"**
5. Status changes to **"Pending Approval"** (yellow border)

#### Review & Sign Off

When work begins:

1. Your permit becomes **"Active"** (shown with blue border)
2. A **designated worker** signs the permit to confirm they're beginning work
3. The **permit issuer** (supervisor) signs to authorize
4. Once both signatures are added, permit becomes **"Completed"**

#### Active Permit Actions

While a permit is active, you can:
- **View** all permit details
- **Add Notes** about progress
- **Upload Evidence** (photos, test results)
- **Hand Over** to another issuer if needed
- **Sign Off** when work is complete

### Using Permit Templates

Recurring work? Save time with templates!

#### Save a Permit as Template

1. Create and fill out a complete permit
2. Before submitting, click **"Save as Template"**
3. Give it a descriptive name (e.g., "Monthly Hot Work - Line Inspection")
4. Click **"Save Template"**

#### Use a Saved Template

1. Click **"Create from Template"**
2. Select the template you want to use
3. Edit any details specific to this job
4. Continue as normal

#### Manage Templates

1. Go to **"Permit Templates"** section
2. View all your saved templates
3. **Edit:** Make changes and re-save (then delete the old version)
4. **Delete:** Remove templates you no longer need

### Permit Status Explained

| Status | Color | Meaning | Next Action |
|--------|-------|---------|-------------|
| Draft | Red | Not yet submitted | Complete and submit |
| Pending Approval | Yellow | Waiting for authorization | Wait for approval |
| Active | Blue | Work is in progress | Monitor and sign off |
| Completed | Green | Work finished & signed off | Archive/reference |

## Company Accreditation

### What is Accreditation?

Accreditation demonstrates that your company meets specific safety, quality, and environmental standards. This is often required by clients and improves your credibility.

### Accreditation Sections

The accreditation form is organized into 25 sections:

1. **Company Details** - Basic business information
2. **Health & Safety Management** - HSE policies and procedures
3. **Environmental Management** - Environmental commitments
4. **Quality Management** - Quality systems (if needed)
5. **Certifications** - ISO, TOTIKA, SiteWise, etc.
... and more

### Starting the Accreditation Process

1. Go to the **Accreditation** tab
2. You'll see an "In Progress" status or an option to "Start Accreditation"
3. Click **"Expand"** to see all sections
4. Sections show progress: 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%

### Completing a Section

#### Step 1: Open the Section

1. Find the section you want to complete
2. Click to expand it
3. Read the title and questions

#### Step 2: Answer Questions

Different sections have different question types:

**Yes/No Questions:**
- Click "Yes" or "No"
- Add supporting details if required

**Scoring Questions (1-4 Scale):**
- Rate from 1 (minimal) to 4 (comprehensive)
- Upload evidence files (PDFs, images)
- Describe your processes in detail

**Certificate Uploads:**
- Click on the green upload box
- Select PDF or image of your certificate
- Expiry date is captured
- View/download the uploaded certificate

#### Step 3: Upload Supporting Evidence

For most questions, you can upload evidence:

1. **Click the upload area** or **paperclip icon**
2. **Select from your computer:**
   - Certificates (PDF)
   - Policies (PDF)
   - Photos of equipment/facilities
   - Inspection reports
3. The file is scanned for:
   - Valid file type (PDF, JPG, PNG, etc.)
   - File size (max 50MB)
   - Images are auto-compressed to prevent large files
4. **Status shows:** ✓ Uploaded

### Certificate Management

#### Upload Certificates

For accredited systems (ISO 9001, Totika, SiteWise, etc.):

1. Check the system checkbox (e.g., "ISO 9001 Certified")
2. Enter the **expiry date** (dd/mm/yyyy format)
3. Click **"View / Download"** to upload the certificate
4. Select your certificate file
5. Certificate is uploaded and linked

#### View/Replace Certificates

1. Uploaded certificates show as **green boxes** with "✓ Certificate Uploaded"
2. Click the **paperclip icon** to:
   - **View/Download** the current certificate
   - **Replace** with a newer version
   - **Delete** if needed

#### Insurance Documents

Similar process for:
- **Public Liability Insurance** (required)
- **Motor Vehicle Insurance** (optional)
- **Professional Indemnity Insurance** (optional)

### Professional Indemnity Insurance

This is an **optional** field in Section 24:

1. Navigate to **Section 24: Insurance**
2. In the **Professional Indemnity Insurance** section:
   - Enter your expiry date
   - Upload your insurance certificate
3. This field is optional - leave blank if not applicable

### Saving Progress

- Your accreditation form is **saved automatically** as you fill it out
- Progress bar shows completion percentage
- You can **return anytime** to continue where you left off
- No need to complete it all in one session

### Final Submission

Once all sections are complete:

1. Click **"Submit for Accreditation Review"**
2. You'll see a summary of all sections
3. Click **"Confirm Submission"**
4. Status changes to "**Submitted for Review**"
5. You'll be notified when approved

## Site Inductions

### What is a Site Induction?

A site induction is a mandatory introduction to a workplace that covers:
- Health and safety rules
- Emergency procedures
- Specific hazards
- Site-specific requirements

### Accessing Inductions

1. Go to the **Inductions** tab
2. You'll see available inductions for sites you've been assigned to
3. Each induction shows:
   - Site name
   - Status (Not Started, In Progress, Completed)
   - Expiry date (when you need to renew)
   - Estimated duration

### Completing an Induction

#### Step 1: Start Induction

1. Click on an induction marked **"Not Started"**
2. Click **"Begin Induction"**
3. You cannot proceed without accepting induction terms

#### Step 2: Work Through Modules

Inductions consist of modules covering:
- **Site Overview** - Map, facilities, key contacts
- **Health & Safety** - Rules and procedures
- **Emergency Response** - Evacuation routes, assembly points
- **Site-Specific Hazards** - Chemical storage, electrical areas, etc.
- **Equipment & Controls** - How to use site equipment safely

For each module:
1. **Read** the content
2. **Watch** embedded videos if included
3. **Answer** any questions asked
4. Click **"Next Module"** when ready

#### Step 3: Final Questions

At the end you may be asked:
- Knowledge check questions (multiple choice)
- Site-specific safety questions
- Confirmation that you understand the requirements

#### Step 4: Sign & Complete

1. **Review** your induction summary
2. **Draw your signature** on the provided pad
   - Use your mouse (desktop) or finger (tablet/mobile)
   - Click "Clear" if you want to start over
   - Click "Done" when satisfied
3. Click **"Complete Induction"**
4. Status changes to **"Completed"** with completion date

### Induction Expiry & Renewal

- Inductions typically **expire after 1 year**
- When expiring soon, you'll see a warning
- Simply retake the induction to renew
- Your previous completion is archived

## Training Records

### What is Training Records?

Training Records tracks all contractor qualifications and training:
- First Aid Certification
- Confined Space Entry Training
- Hot Work Certification
- Forklift Operation
- Safety Inductions
- Any company-specific training

### Viewing Your Training Records

1. Go to the **Training Records** tab
2. See all your current training and certifications
3. For each record, you can see:
   - Training type/name
   - Date completed
   - Expiry date (if applicable)
   - Certificate/evidence (if uploaded)
   - Status (Current, Expired, Expiring Soon)

### Adding Training Records

1. Click **"+ Add Training Record"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Training Type** - What training did you complete?
   - **Date Completed** - When did you finish? (dd/mm/yyyy)
   - **Expiry Date** - When does it expire? (dd/mm/yyyy, if applicable)
   - **Provider** - Who provided the training? (e.g., "Safety Training Ltd")
   - **Certificate/Evidence** - Upload a file (PDF, image, etc.)
3. Click **"Save Training Record"**

### Uploading Certificates

When uploading evidence:
- **Accepted formats:** PDF, JPG, PNG, GIF
- **Max size:** 50MB per file
- **Image compression:** Large images are automatically compressed
- **Visibility:** The file is securely stored and only visible to authorized personnel

### Managing Expired Training

- **Expired training** appears with a red warning
- **Expiring within 30 days** appears yellow
- **Current training** appears green
- Schedule renewal training before expiry dates
- Contact your training provider to renew

## Job Safety Analysis (JSEA)

### What is a JSEA?

A Job Safety and Environmental Analysis (JSEA) is a systematic approach to:
- Break down work into steps
- Identify hazards for each step
- Specify appropriate control measures
- Ensure work is done safely

### Accessing JSEA

1. Go to the **JSEA** tab
2. You'll see:
   - **Templates** - Pre-built JSEA templates for common tasks
   - **Your JSEAs** - Previous JSEA documents you've created

### Creating a JSEA

#### Step 1: Choose Your Approach

**Option A: From Template**
1. Click **"Load Template"**
2. Select a template for your task (e.g., "Pipe Installation")
3. Review the job steps and hazards already listed
4. Customize for your specific job

**Option B: Create Custom**
1. Click **"Create Custom JSEA"**
2. Enter a title (e.g., "Foundation Excavation - Site A")
3. Describe the work scope
4. Add date and location

#### Step 2: Define Job Steps

1. Click **"Add Step"** for each major work phase
2. For each step, enter:
   - **Step Name** - Short description (e.g., "Excavate trench")
   - **Who Does It?** - Worker role/position
   - **Equipment Needed** - Tools, machinery, PPE
3. You can **reorder steps** by dragging

#### Step 3: Identify Hazards for Each Step

1. Under each step, click **"Add Hazard"**
2. Enter the **hazard** (e.g., "Electrocution from buried cables")
3. Select **risk rating:**
   - 🔴 **Critical** - Stop work immediately
   - 🟠 **High** - Requires senior approval
   - 🟡 **Medium** - Standard controls needed
   - 🟢 **Low** - Routine precautions

#### Step 4: Specify Controls

For each hazard, specify:
- **Engineering Controls** - Eliminate the hazard (e.g., "Isolate the circuit")
- **Administrative Controls** - Rules and procedures (e.g., "Only licensed electrician can work")
- **PPE** - Personal protective equipment (e.g., "Insulated gloves, helmet")

#### Step 5: Review & Save

1. Review all steps, hazards, and controls
2. Check that **all critical/high risks** have controls
3. Click **"Save JSEA"**
4. Give it a memorable name

### Saving as Template

If you've created a JSEA you use repeatedly:

1. Open your completed JSEA
2. Click **"Save as Template"**
3. Give it a descriptive name
4. Future JSEAs can use this template

### Using a JSEA

1. Keep your JSEA on-site during work
2. Review steps and hazards before starting each phase
3. Ensure all team members understand controls
4. Update JSEA if conditions change (new hazards discovered)
5. Keep completed JSEA for records

## Tips & Best Practices

### Best Practices for Permits

✅ **DO:**
- Complete permits **before starting work**
- Be thorough when answering safety questions
- Upload relevant evidence and test results
- Review hazards and controls with your team
- Keep permits for audit purposes

❌ **DON'T:**
- Start work without an approved permit
- Leave permits incomplete or vague
- Ignore safety questions - answer truthfully
- Approve work you're not confident about
- Discard permits until audit trail is complete

### Best Practices for Accreditation

✅ **DO:**
- Complete sections systematically
- Upload current, valid certificates
- Be honest about your capabilities
- Include evidence of your processes
- Keep certificates current before expiry

❌ **DON'T:**
- Upload expired certificates
- Leave required fields blank
- Overstate your certifications
- Forget to renew certifications before expiry
- Submit unreviewed forms

### Best Practices for Safety

✅ **DO:**
- Treat inductions seriously - they save lives
- Keep training current
- Share JSEA findings with your team
- Report hazards immediately
- Update documents when procedures change

❌ **DON'T:**
- Skip inductions to save time
- Work beyond your training/certification
- Ignore environmental/safety concerns
- Rely on memory - document everything
- Assume hazards don't apply to "experienced" workers

### File Upload Tips

**File Format:**
- **Certificates:** PDF files are best
- **Photos:** JPG or PNG format
- **Mixed files:** The system accepts all common formats

**File Size:**
- Large images are automatically compressed
- Maximum individual file size: 50MB
- If upload fails, check file size

**Naming Files:**
- Use clear, descriptive names: "ISO9001_Cert_2023-2026.pdf"
- Include dates for time-sensitive documents
- Avoid special characters that might cause issues

### Date Entry

Dates are entered in **dd/mm/yyyy** format:

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| 25/12/2026 | 25th December 2026 |
| 01/06/2025 | 1st June 2025 |
| 15/03/2024 | 15th March 2024 |

### Screen Refresh

This clears your browser cache and shows the latest updates.

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues & Solutions

#### I can't log in

**Problem:** "Invalid email or password" error

**Solutions:**
1. Check that your **email is correct** - no extra spaces
2. **CAPS LOCK** is off when entering password
3. Use **"Forgot Password"** to reset it
4. Try a different browser
5. Clear your browser cookies and try again

**If still not working:** Contact your administrator

---

#### Files won't upload

**Problem:** Upload fails or hangs

**Solutions:**
1. Check **file size** - must be under 50MB
2. Check **file format** - use PDF or standard image format
3. Ensure **stable internet connection**
4. Try uploading a **smaller file first** (e.g., a photo)
5. Try a **different browser**
6. Clear browser cache and refresh

**Still failing?** Try uploading later - the system may be temporarily busy.

---

#### Signatures aren't saving

**Problem:** Signature pad appears but won't work

**Solutions:**
1. Click **"Clear"** and try drawing again
2. Use **smooth, slower movements**
3. On mobile, ensure you're using your **finger**, not a stylus (unless it's capacitive)
4. Try on a **desktop/laptop** for easier drawing
5. Ensure your device/mouse is clean

**If on mobile:** Tap and hold, then draw without lifting your finger.

---

#### Data isn't saving

**Problem:** I filled in the form but my changes are gone

**Solutions:**
1. **Accreditation:** Data is auto-saved. Wait 2 seconds after typing.
2. **Permits:** Click **"Save Draft"** or **"Save Changes"** explicitly
3. Check your **internet connection** - you need to be online to save
4. Try **refreshing the page** - your data may be there
5. Try a **different browser** or device

**Prevention:** After important changes, click Save and wait for confirmation message.

---

#### Can't find an old permit or record

**Problem:** "I can't find my permit from last month"

**Solutions:**
1. Check different **tabs** - might be in Archives
2. Use **search function** if available
3. Try **scrolling down** - may be below current view
4. Permits expire from view after a certain period
5. Try **different date filters** or **status filters**

**Best practice:** Keep a personal log of permit IDs for important work.

---

#### Date format confusion

**Problem:** System shows date as "17/03/2025" but I need it as "03/17/2025"

**Solution:** 
ContractorHQ uses the **dd/mm/yyyy** format consistently (New Zealand/UK standard):
- **Day first** (01-31)
- **Month second** (01-12)
- **Year last** (2024, 2025, etc.)

Example: The 3rd of May 2026 is entered as **03/05/2026**

---

#### "Object Not Found" error on certificates

**Problem:** Certificate link shows "Object Not Found" or broken image

**Solutions:**
1. Try **clearing your browser cache** (Ctrl+Shift+R)
2. **Re-upload the certificate** - the old link may be broken
3. Ensure you're **still logged in** and have access rights
4. Try **opening in a new private/incognito window**

**If persistent:** The file may have been deleted. Re-upload it.
